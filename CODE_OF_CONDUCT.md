# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We pledge that everyone participating in the **Sysadmin Security Lab** project and our community will be treated with respect and dignity, regardless of age, body size, caste, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are committed to building and maintaining a safe, inclusive, and respectful community.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Helping others learn and grow
- Respecting security and privacy concerns

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination
- Offensive comments related to gender, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, mental illness, neurodivergence, physical appearance, body size, race, ethnicity, national origin, religion, caste, or other protected statuses
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Deliberate intimidation or threats
- Stalking or following without consent
- Photography or recording without consent
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others' private information (doxxing)
- Advocating for, or encouraging, any of the above behaviors
- Disruption of community events or forums
- Malicious hacking or unauthorized access to systems

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces, including:

- GitHub issues, pull requests, and discussions
- Project documentation and communication channels
- Community events and meetings
- Third-party spaces managed on the project's behalf

This Code of Conduct also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces. Examples of representing a project include using an official project e-mail address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.

---

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by contacting the project maintainers at **security@solo2121.com** or via GitHub issues. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

Project maintainers are obligated to:

- Maintain confidentiality of the reporter
- Review and investigate all complaints
- Respond to reporters with updates on the investigation
- Take appropriate action to address violations

---

## Consequences

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in:

1. **Warning**: A private message clarifying the concerns and expectations
2. **Temporary Ban**: Temporary removal from community spaces (7–30 days)
3. **Permanent Ban**: Permanent removal from the project and community spaces

Repeated violations, especially severe ones (harassment, hate speech, threats), will result in immediate permanent removal without warning.

---

## Appeal Process

If you believe a decision was made in error, you may appeal to the project maintainers within 14 days of the decision. Appeals should include:

- Your name and contact information
- Description of the incident
- Explanation of why you believe the decision was incorrect
- Any additional context or evidence
